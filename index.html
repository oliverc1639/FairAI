Detected Clause Types

Generic, pre-approved explanations (mock)

ARB Arbitration requirement
See
What it usually means: Disputes are typically resolved through arbitration instead of court.
  • May limit the ability to sue in court or participate in class actions.
  • May require disputes to be handled privately with an arbitrator.
FEE Late fees / penalty language
See
What it usually means: Payments after a specified date may trigger fees or penalties.
  • May add a flat fee and/or daily interest-like charges.
  • May create additional consequences after repeated late payments.
REN Automatic renewal
See
What it usually means: The agreement renews unless you cancel within a notice period.
  • May require cancellation before a deadline (e.g., 30 days notice).
  • May continue billing or obligations into a new term.
TERM Termination conditions
See
What it usually means: Rules for ending the agreement (notice, fees, causes).
  • May impose fees for early termination.
  • May require written notice and specific delivery methods.
WAIV Liability waiver / limitation
See
What it usually means: Limits responsibility for damages under certain conditions.
  • May reduce compensation available for certain losses.
  • May exclude indirect, incidental, or consequential damages.
RENT Rent increase notice
See
What it usually means: Describes if/when rent can change and how notice is given.
  • May allow increases after a set term or with written notice.
  • May specify timing, delivery, and effective dates.
