Background info
H-1B is an employer-filed petition for a specialty occupation worker. The employer files Form I-129 (H-1B supplement + fees) with USCIS. The worker’s status is tied to the petitioning employer and the job details (role, wage, worksite). This tool flags issues that can affect you: fees/cost shifting, strict filing deadlines, job changes that trigger amendments, and “status risk” after termination.

Potentially harmful clause / risk triggers (H-1B)

• COST SHIFTING: Some filing/attorney fees cannot be charged to the worker via payroll deduction or repayment clauses. • DEADLINES: Extensions must be filed before the current H-1B end date; a timely filed extension can allow continued work up to 240 days while pending. • JOB CHANGES: Title/duties/worksite changes may require an amended petition; working outside the approved terms risks status. • TERMINATION RISK: Up to a 60-day discretionary grace period may be available (or until the petition end date, whichever is shorter).
